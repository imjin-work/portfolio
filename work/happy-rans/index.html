Visualizing empowerment and visibility through campaign

In collaboration with Amnesty and the Transgender Liberation Front, our team and I developed a campaign combining offline guerrilla events, advertisements, and a dedicated website. I created illustrations using bold black lines and a reinterpreted transgender symbol with heightened saturation, conveying a message of celebration and inclusion while emphasizing strength and visibility.

View Live Website

Project Goal

Raising visibility and awareness of transgender lives in Korean society

In Korea, transgender individuals face ongoing discrimination, hate crimes, and social invisibility. The goal of the campaign was to honor those who have been lost while bringing visibility to those living within society today — highlighting their existence, resilience, and right to be seen and recognized.

Solution

Transforming awareness into action through a nationwide guerrilla campaign

To make the shopping experience more intuitive for Korean users, the layout was restructured using navigation patterns and button placements familiar in local e-commerce. Korean typeface with visual characteristics similar to Floyd’s original English font was selected, maintaining consistency while ensuring legibility and cultural alignment. The result is a localized experience that feels both authentic to the brand and natural to Korean consumers.

Visual Conecpt

Amplifying visibility through bold illustration and graphic clarity

To align with the campaign’s goal of making transgender lives visible, illustrations were designed with emphasized outlines and strong visual contrast to ensure clarity and impact. The graphic system itself was created to be bold and attention-grabbing, allowing the campaign to stand out in both digital and physical spaces while reinforcing its message of visibility and recognition.

Website

Extending the campaign’s message into the digital space

The website served as an online extension of the campaign, providing factual information on discrimination and hate crimes against transgender individuals, alongside personal interviews that offered authentic voices and perspectives. It also included a section where supporters could submit messages of solidarity, allowing the campaign’s impact to expand beyond physical installations and foster a shared space of empathy and connection online.

Impact

Driving visibility and public awareness through collective participation

Over 3,000 messages of support were collected through the campaign’s digital platform, reflecting widespread engagement and solidarity. The project was featured in over 10 media outlets, helping raise public awareness of transgender issues and sparking broader conversations around visibility and equality in Korean society.
