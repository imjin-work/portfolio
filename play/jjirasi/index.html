Questioning the weight of political promises through motion design

During the presidential election, politicians flooded the public with grand promises — words that often felt as fleeting as flyers scattered on the streets. In Korea, such flyers are colloquially called “jjirasi,” a slang term referring to cheaply printed sheets that spread rumors or unreliable information. I created a website featuring motion posters for each candidate designed in this style, visually echoing the hollowness of political rhetoric and the ease with which words lose their weight.
